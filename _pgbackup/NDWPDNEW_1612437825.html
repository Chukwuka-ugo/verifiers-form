Welcome to NDWPD

Noble Delta Women for Peace and Development International (NDWPD), formerly known as Niger Delta Women’s movement for Peace and Development is a non- governmental organization in special consultative Status with the United Nations ECOSOC, accredited to the United Nations Environmental Program (UNEP) and the Green Climate Fund based in Delta state with a vision to empower the Niger Delta Women and the most vulnerable people in the region and for the promotion and realization of their rights.

The vision of NDWPD is to see A Society that respects Women Human rights, Gender Equality, Women Empowerment, Peace and Free of Gender Based Violence. We envision a world for women to live in where there is Social and Environmental Justice, Gender Equality and respect for women's human rights.

Latest News

The Covid-19 pandemic no doubt stalled many of our earlier planned activities for the year but despite the setbacks, Noble Delta Women for Peace and Development Int 'l(NDWPD) is finishing strong by hosting two major events on December 3,2020(Virtual) and December 4,2020(Hybrid) and with plans to host another capacity building project on Stopkillerrobots for Civil Society Organizations across the country on 21st January, 2021.

Virtual Awareness Campaign to Stop Killer Robots held on 3rd December, 2020
The Virtual Awareness Campaign to call for the Ban of Killer Robots and and was done on Dec 3rd,2020, with the special adviser to the Delta State Governor on security and Peace Building, Chief Edwin Uzor JP as guest speakers while the Co-leads of the Stop killer robots campaign in Nigeria, Amb. Caroline Usikpedo, Executive Director of Noble Delta Women for Peace and Development Int’l (host organization) and Mrs Mimidoo Achakpa, Executive Director, International Action Network on Small arms (IANSA), as well as Executive Director, NEFEROK, Mrs. Ayo N. Okotie were all speakers.

All participants at the virtual event understood the threat killer robots pose and the need for Nigeria to join the league of other countries that have endorsed a ban of fully autonomous weapons. The Campaign to stop killer robots of which NDWPD is a member was formed in October 2012 and it's a growing global coalition of 171 international, regional, and National non-governmental organizations in 65 countries that are working to retain meaningful human control over the use of force and pre-emptively ban fully autonomous weapons. In the year 2018, Non-Allied Movements in March called for a “legally binding international instrument” stipulating prohibitions and regulations on lethal autonomous weapons systems. In April 2018, a group of African States recommended concluding a legally binding instrument at the earliest and found that “fully autonomous weapons systems or laws that are not under human control should be banned”. NAM and its 125 member States have continued to call for a ban since 2018 and the campaign encourages national statements affirming support for this goals.

Since 2013 a total of 93 Countries have publicly expressed the views and concern on fully autonomous weapons and NDWPD which is also a member of the campaign has been speaking on the topic. This Virtual Awareness campaign held on 3rd December 2020 is therefore a consolidation of our earlier position as well strengthening and expansion of our existing networks .The virtual awareness campaign ended with a call to action which saw all participants making an affirmation to be committed in raising awareness on stopkillerrobots till our voices are heard and the Nigerian government endorses a ban.

GNDR 2nd National Coordination Meeting held on 4th December, 2020
The other event, the Global Network for Civil Society Organizations for Disaster Reduction (GNDR) 2nd National Coordination Meeting was held on 4th December 2020 and was a huge success with delegates from the 6 geopolitical zones in the country all in attendance. The meeting which was hybrid began with a courtesy call on the the State Emergency Management Agency(SEMA), where the permanent secretary, Mr Karo Ovemeso, made a commitment for the SEMA to always collaborate with GNDR. The theme of the programme was Building Resilience and Strategies for implementing the New GNDR Strategic Plan in Nigeria. Each CSOs spoke on their works and how far they have gone in achieving their goals. High point of the meeting was the presentation of the new GNDR strategic plan and how it can be achieved in Nigeria by the GNDR focal point in Nigeria, Amb.Caroline Usikpedo, the Executive Director of Noble Delta Women for Peace and Development Int'l.
